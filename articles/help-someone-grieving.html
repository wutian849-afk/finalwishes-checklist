Why This Matters

Whether you are planning ahead for yourself or handling arrangements for a loved one, having a structured approach reduces stress, saves money, and ensures nothing is missed. The average funeral costs $7,000-$12,000 in the United States. Making decisions under emotional pressure often leads to overspending and overlooked details.

Key Steps to Follow

1. Take a Deep Breath

The first step is simply to pause and take stock. There is no need to do everything at once. Our checklists break down each area into manageable tasks that you can complete at your own pace.

2. Gather Important Information

Start collecting relevant documents, contact information, and preferences. If you're handling arrangements for someone else, check if they left any written instructions, a will, or preplanned funeral arrangements.

3. Work Through Each Section

Use our specialized checklists to systematically address each area:

  • Immediate steps after a death
  • Funeral and memorial arrangements
  • Estate and legal processes
  • Financial accounts and insurance
  • Digital accounts and online presence
  • Notifications and documentation

4. Document Everything

Keep written records of all decisions, receipts, and correspondence. This will be invaluable for estate administration, tax purposes, and family communication.

5. Give Yourself Time

Grief is not linear. Allow yourself and your family the time and space to process. Our checklists are designed to be worked through over days and weeks, not hours.
